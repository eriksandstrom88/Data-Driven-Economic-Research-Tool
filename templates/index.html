Choose 5 Series to Compare

Use the dropdown menus below the chart to select up to 5 data series to display on the chart. For reference, the numbers in the dropdowns are the index numbers of different series that can be found in the index table at the bottom of the page.

The summary statistic tables will automatically populate based on the data that is selected. The summary tables will adjust according to the chart zooming into a certain range, or panning once zoomed. To return to the original chart with its full date range, click on the house icon in the chart's tool bar top right.

Individual line plots can be turned on or off by clicking on the legend below the chart.

Compare Two Series


Use the dropdown menus to the right of the scatter plot to select two series to compare. Like above, the numbers in the dropdowns are the index numbers of different series that can be found in the index table at the bottom of the page. Additionally, you can select "change" or "percent change" to examine how the selected series changes from interval to interval

The gauge will display the correlation coefficient between the two series, as well as the R^2 score which represents how much of the variance in variable 2 can be accounted for by variable 1.

Also similar to above, the user may zoom in on a selection of the two series, and the correlation and R^2 will re-calculate.

FORECASTING MODELS


There are three models below that will forecast, respectively, Consumer Price Index (CPI), Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE), and GDP Deflator.

Each model contains 10 inputs for predicting the relevant measure of inflation. The user can input a forecast for any and all predictor variables, or simply play "what if" to see what happens if a certain variable changes by any amount. Any variables that are not input by the user will default to the "most recent" value reported in the table. Once values are input, click the button to forcast inflation!
